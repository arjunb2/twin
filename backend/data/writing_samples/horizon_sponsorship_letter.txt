We're reaching out from Team Horizon, a student team from the Cochin University of Science and Technology (CUSAT) that designs and builds fully autonomous Mars rovers. Our latest rover, Tiranga 2.0, has been developed entirely in-house and is capable of performing tasks such as autonomous navigation, terrain exploration, sampling, and system diagnostics in simulated Martian environments.

Over the past year, our journey has taken us to the global stage. We represented India at the European Rover Challenge 2024 in Kraków, Poland, where we secured an 18th global rank, proudly standing as the only team from South India at the event.

Continuing this momentum, we also participated in the International Rover Challenge 2026, where we achieved a world rank of 25. These milestones reflect the consistency, technical depth, and dedication our team brings to every competition.

We are now working towards the next iteration of our rover, aiming to push our capabilities even further. As we take this step forward, we are seeking support from organizations that believe in innovation, student-led research, and global representation.
