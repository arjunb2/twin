Latest Space Highlights | March 2026

ISRO SUCCESSFULLY TESTS POWERFUL CRYOGENIC ENGINE
The Indian Space Research Organisation recently conducted a successful hot test of the CE-20 cryogenic engine, achieving a thrust level of 22 tonnes. The test was carried out at the ISRO Propulsion Complex and marks a major step forward for India's heavy-lift launch capabilities.
Cryogenic engines are extremely important for modern rockets because they provide higher efficiency and allow rockets to carry heavier payloads into orbit. This development strengthens India's launch vehicle technology and supports upcoming missions that require greater payload capacity.

INDIA PLANS FUTURE MOON MISSIONS: CHANDRAYAAN-4 AND 5
Following the success of Chandrayaan-3, ISRO has revealed plans for Chandrayaan-4 and Chandrayaan-5, two ambitious missions aimed at expanding India's lunar exploration program.
Chandrayaan-4 is expected to focus on collecting lunar samples and bringing them back to Earth, a major technological challenge attempted by only a few space agencies. Chandrayaan-5 may include a heavier 350-kg rover with a mission life of about 100 days, enabling longer and more detailed scientific studies on the Moon's surface.

NASA SATELLITE RETURNS TO EARTH AFTER 14 YEARS
After spending nearly 14 years studying Earth's radiation belts, NASA's Van Allen Probe A re-entered Earth's atmosphere in March 2026.
The spacecraft burned up mostly during reentry over the Pacific Ocean, posing almost no risk to people on Earth. During its mission, the probe helped scientists understand space weather and the behavior of charged particles around Earth, discoveries that continue to influence satellite design and astronaut safety.

NASA BEGINS BUILDING DRAGONFLY MISSION TO TITAN
NASA has started building Dragonfly, a nuclear-powered rotorcraft that will explore Titan, Saturn's largest moon.
Scheduled for launch in 2028, Dragonfly will fly between different locations on Titan's surface to study its chemistry, atmosphere, and geology. Scientists believe Titan may contain complex organic molecules that could provide clues about how life might begin on other worlds.

SPACE FACT OF THE ISSUE
Titan is one of the most Earth-like worlds in the solar system. It has rivers, lakes, clouds, and rain but instead of water, these features are made of liquid methane and ethane. Because Titan has a thick atmosphere and complex chemistry, scientists believe it may resemble what early Earth looked like billions of years ago, making it a key location for studying the origins of life.

QUICK ORBIT UPDATE
Space agencies worldwide are increasing investments in Moon exploration and deep-space missions. New propulsion technologies such as advanced cryogenic engines are improving rocket efficiency. Robotic explorers like Dragonfly are opening a new era of flying exploration on other worlds.
